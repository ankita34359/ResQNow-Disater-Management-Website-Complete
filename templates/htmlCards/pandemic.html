  • Definition: A pandemic is the worldwide spread of a new disease affecting a large number of people.
  • Examples: COVID-19, Spanish Flu, H1N1, and more.
  • Scope: Global â€” unlike epidemics which are regional.
  • Impact: Healthcare systems, economies, travel, mental health, and daily life.
  • Transmission: Often spread through respiratory droplets, surface contact, or close interaction.

â›” What NOT To Do During a Pandemic?

  • Do NOT: Spread misinformation or rely on unverified sources.
  • Do NOT: Ignore public health advisories or skip quarantine rules.
  • Do NOT: Hoard essential supplies unnecessarily.
  • Do NOT: Assume you're immune or can't be affected.
  • Do NOT: Neglect hygiene or mask guidelines.

âœ… How to Prepare for a Pandemic?

  • Stay Informed: Follow updates from WHO, CDC, or local health authorities.
  • Emergency Supplies: Stock up on essentials (medications, food, hygiene).
  • Medical Records: Keep digital and physical copies ready.
  • Work Plans: Set up remote work/study systems and backup plans.
  • Social Planning: Prepare communication strategies with family and loved ones.

ðŸ§¼ What TO DO During a Pandemic?

  • Wear Masks: Especially in crowded or indoor public places.
  • Hand Hygiene: Wash hands frequently with soap for 20+ seconds.
  • Social Distance: Maintain 6 feet distance from others where possible.
  • Boost Immunity: Maintain healthy diet, rest, and stress-free routine.
  • Follow Rules: Comply with travel, quarantine, and testing protocols.
