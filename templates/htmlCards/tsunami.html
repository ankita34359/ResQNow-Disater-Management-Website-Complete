🌊 Tsunami Preparedness

Be alert, act fast — know what to do before, during, and after a tsunami.

🌊 What is a Tsunami?

  • Definition: A series of ocean waves caused by large underwater disturbances such as earthquakes, volcanic eruptions, or landslides.
  • Speed: Can travel up to 500–800 km/h in deep water, slowing near coasts but increasing in height.
  • Impact: Severe flooding, destruction of infrastructure, and significant loss of life if unprepared.

🚫 What Should You NOT Do During a Tsunami?

  • Do NOT: Stay near the shore to watch the waves.
  • Do NOT: Return to the affected area until authorities declare it safe.
  • Do NOT: Rely only on visual signs — always respond to warnings.
  • Do NOT: Use bridges or roads near the ocean — they may collapse or be underwater.

✅ What TO DO Before a Tsunami?

  • Know Warning Signs: Sudden ocean withdrawal, loud roaring sound, or a strong coastal earthquake.
  • Emergency Plan: Have an evacuation route and meeting point ready.
  • Emergency Kit: Include food, water, flashlight, radio, and documents in waterproof containers.
  • Stay Informed: Sign up for alerts and warnings from local authorities or tsunami warning centers.

🏃‍♂️ What TO DO During a Tsunami?

  • Move Inland: Evacuate to higher ground or as far from the shore as possible.
  • Stay Away: Avoid rivers and estuaries connected to the ocean.
  • Listen to Authorities: Follow emergency broadcast instructions.
  • Be Ready for Multiple Waves: The first wave is not always the largest.

🔧 What TO DO After a Tsunami?

  • Wait for Official Clearance: Do not return home until the "all-clear" is given.
  • Stay Away from Debris: Sharp objects, unstable structures, and contaminated water can pose dangers.
  • Check for Injuries: Administer first aid and seek help if needed.
  • Inspect Utilities: Avoid using electrical devices near water; report gas leaks immediately.

📦 Tsunami Emergency Kit Essentials

  • Water: 1 gallon/person/day for at least 3 days.
  • Food: Non-perishable, ready-to-eat supplies.
  • Clothing: Extra clothes, raincoat, and sturdy shoes.
  • Flashlight: With extra batteries or solar charging.
  • Communication: Battery-operated or hand-crank radio.
  • Documents: IDs, insurance, and emergency contacts in waterproof bag.
