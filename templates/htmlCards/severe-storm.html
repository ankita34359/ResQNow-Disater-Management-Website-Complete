Understanding severe storms: what to do before, during, and after a storm hits.

üå©Ô∏è What is a Severe Storm?

  • Definition: A severe storm refers to intense weather conditions, such as heavy rain, hail, high winds, and lightning, that can cause damage and pose threats to life and property.
  • Types: Thunderstorms, hailstorms, windstorms, and tornadoes.
  • Signs: Dark skies, sudden drop in temperature, heavy rainfall, or a sudden increase in wind speed.
  • Impact: Flash floods, fallen trees, power outages, property damage, and personal injury.

‚õî What NOT to Do During a Severe Storm?

  • Do NOT: Stay outside or attempt to drive through flooded areas.
  • Do NOT: Take shelter under trees or near windows, as they could break during high winds.
  • Do NOT: Use electrical appliances or plug in devices during lightning storms to avoid electrocution.
  • Do NOT: Ignore weather warnings or neglect to evacuate if advised by local authorities.

‚úÖ What to Do Before a Severe Storm?

  • Stay Informed: Monitor weather alerts from official sources such as the National Weather Service (NWS).
  • Prepare an Emergency Kit: Include non-perishable food, water, medications, and flashlights.
  • Secure the Property: Trim trees, secure outdoor items, and close all windows and doors.
  • Evacuation Plan: Plan your evacuation route and know local shelters.
  • Check Insulation: Ensure that your roof, windows, and doors are well-insulated to resist storm damage.

üßØ What to Do During a Severe Storm?

  • Seek Shelter: Stay indoors and away from windows. Take cover in a basement or interior room.
  • Stay Calm: Keep yourself and others calm, and avoid panic.
  • Avoid Electricity: Unplug electrical appliances to prevent damage during power surges.
  • Stay Low: If you're in an area prone to tornadoes or flooding, stay low and take shelter in a bathtub or under sturdy furniture.
  • Listen to Alerts: Keep a battery-powered radio or smartphone nearby to listen to weather updates and evacuation instructions.

üì¶ Emergency Kit for Storms

  • Water and Food: At least 3 days of non-perishable food and water for each family member.
  • Flashlights & Batteries: Enough lighting in case of power outages.
  • First-Aid Kit: Bandages, pain relievers, disinfectants, and essential medical supplies.
  • Portable Charger: For charging phones and communication devices.
  • Clothing: Warm clothing, blankets, and rain gear.

üí™ How to Recover After a Severe Storm?

  • Stay Safe: Wait for official clearance before returning home after the storm.
  • Inspect Damage: Look for roof leaks, broken windows, or downed power lines.
  • Contact Insurance: Report damages to your insurance provider.
  • Assist Others: Help neighbors, especially the elderly or vulnerable, who may need assistance with recovery.
  • Stay Informed: Keep monitoring weather reports for any further threats.
