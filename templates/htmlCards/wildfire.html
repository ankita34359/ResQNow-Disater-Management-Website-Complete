üî• What is a Wildfire?

  • Definition: An uncontrolled fire that spreads rapidly through vegetation, forests, or grasslands.
  • Causes: Lightning, unattended campfires, discarded cigarettes, arson, or power lines.
  • Impact: Destroys natural habitats, homes, air quality, and can be fatal if uncontained.
  • Season: Most common in dry seasons, especially in areas with hot, dry, and windy weather.

‚õî What NOT To Do During a Wildfire?

  • Do NOT: Stay in your home unless directed by authorities ‚Äî evacuate early.
  • Do NOT: Drive through heavy smoke or blocked roads.
  • Do NOT: Use water sources (like hoses) unnecessarily if facing evacuation.
  • Do NOT: Ignore air quality warnings and stay outdoors for long periods.

‚úÖ What TO DO Before a Wildfire?

  • Create Defensible Space: Clear dry leaves, debris, and flammable materials around your home.
  • Emergency Kit: Include N95 masks, goggles, food, water, medication, and documents.
  • Evacuation Plan: Know at least two ways out of your neighborhood.
  • Stay Informed: Sign up for emergency alerts and wildfire tracking apps.
  • Prepare Vehicles: Keep fuel tanks full and vehicles ready to go.

üèÉ What TO DO During a Wildfire?

  • Evacuate Early: Don't wait for mandatory orders if the fire is close.
  • Stay Low: If trapped, stay near the ground to avoid smoke inhalation.
  • Cover Up: Wear long sleeves, goggles, and a mask to protect yourself.
  • Shelter Indoors: If stuck, stay in a cleared area with minimal vegetation.

üßØ What TO DO After a Wildfire?

  • Wait for Clearance: Return home only when authorities confirm it's safe.
  • Check Property: Inspect for structural damage, hot spots, and downed lines.
  • Protect Lungs: Continue using masks ‚Äî ash and soot may still linger in the air.
  • Document Losses: Photograph damages for insurance and relief processes.

üì¶ Wildfire Emergency Kit Essentials

  • Respiratory Protection: N95 masks, wet cloths, and eye protection.
  • Food & Water: 3-day supply minimum, especially sealed bottled water.
  • Fire Tools: Fire extinguisher, shovel, gloves, and fire-resistant blankets.
  • Digital Backup: USB or cloud backup of important documents.
  • Communication: Battery radio, fully charged phone with contact list.
