üíß What is a Flood?

  • Definition: An overflow of water onto normally dry land, caused by heavy rainfall, river overflow, dam failure, or snowmelt.
  • Types: Flash floods, river floods, urban floods, coastal floods.
  • Impact: Property damage, contaminated water, infrastructure failure, displacement of people.

üõë What Should You NOT Do During a Flood?

  • Do NOT: Walk, swim, or drive through floodwaters.
  • Do NOT: Use electrical appliances if wet or standing in water.
  • Do NOT: Cross bridges over fast-moving water.
  • Do NOT: Touch downed power lines or objects in contact with them.

‚úÖ What TO DO Before a Flood?

  • Know Your Risk: Check flood maps and community alerts.
  • Emergency Kit: Pack essentials like water, food, flashlight, and medication.
  • Safeguard Your Home: Elevate electrical equipment, seal basements, install check valves.
  • Insurance: Get flood insurance ‚Äî regular home insurance doesn't cover flood damage.

üè† What TO DO During a Flood?

  • Evacuate: Leave immediately if instructed by authorities.
  • Move to Higher Ground: Avoid low-lying areas, rivers, and streams.
  • Stay Informed: Use local alerts, radio, or apps for emergency updates.
  • Turn Off Utilities: Shut off gas, electricity, and water if safe to do so.

üîß What TO DO After a Flood?

  • Wait for Official Word: Return only when declared safe.
  • Document Damage: Take photos for insurance claims.
  • Protect Yourself: Wear protective gear when cleaning up.
  • Sanitize: Disinfect all items in contact with floodwater.
  • Check Utilities: Have professionals inspect gas, water, and electrical systems.

üì¶ Emergency Flood Kit Checklist

  • Water: 1 gallon per person per day for 3 days.
  • Food: Non-perishable, easy-to-prepare items.
  • First Aid Kit: Bandages, antiseptics, pain relievers.
  • Tools: Flashlight, extra batteries, multi-tool, whistle.
  • Documents: Copies of ID, insurance, and medical records in waterproof bag.
  • Cash: In small bills, as ATMs may not work.
