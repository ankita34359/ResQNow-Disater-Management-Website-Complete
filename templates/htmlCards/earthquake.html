üåÄ What is an Earthquake?

An earthquake is the sudden shaking of the Earth's surface caused by the release of energy from the Earth's crust. This release is typically due to tectonic plate movements and can lead to significant ground shaking and potential damage.

‚úÖ What Should You Do During an Earthquake?

  • Drop, Cover, and Hold On: Immediately drop to your hands and knees, cover your head and neck under sturdy furniture, and hold on until the shaking stops.
  • Stay Indoors: If you're inside, remain there. Move away from windows and exterior walls.
  • If Outside: Move to an open area away from buildings, trees, and power lines.
  • If Driving: Pull over to a clear area, stop, and stay inside the vehicle until the shaking ceases.

‚ùå What Should You Avoid During an Earthquake?

  • Do Not Run Outside: Staying indoors is safer as exterior hazards like falling debris pose significant risks.
  • Avoid Elevators: Use stairs if evacuation is necessary after the shaking stops.
  • Stay Away from Windows: Glass can shatter and cause injuries.
  • Don't Stand in Doorways: Contrary to old advice, doorways are not the safest places during an earthquake.

üì¶ How to Prepare Before an Earthquake?

  • Secure Heavy Items: Anchor bookshelves, cabinets, and other heavy furniture to walls.
  • Emergency Kit: Assemble a kit with essentials like water, food, flashlight, batteries, and first aid supplies.
  • Develop a Family Plan: Establish a communication plan and designate safe meeting spots.
  • Structural Safety: Ensure your home complies with seismic building codes and retrofit if necessary.

üö® What to Do After an Earthquake?

  • Check for Injuries: Provide first aid and seek medical attention if needed.
  • Inspect for Hazards: Look for gas leaks, damaged electrical lines, and structural damage.
  • Be Prepared for Aftershocks: These can follow the main quake and cause additional damage.
  • Stay Informed: Listen to emergency broadcasts and follow official instructions.
