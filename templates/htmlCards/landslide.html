  • Definition: A rapid downward movement of rock, earth, or debris on a slope.
  • Causes: Heavy rainfall, earthquakes, volcanic activity, human activity (like deforestation and construction).
  • Types: Rock falls, debris flows, mudslides, and slumps.
  • Risk Areas: Mountainous terrain, areas with loose soil, and construction on steep slopes.

üö´ What Should You NOT Do During a Landslide?

  • Do NOT: Ignore warning signs like cracks, bulging ground, or tilting trees/fences.
  • Do NOT: Attempt to cross flooded or unstable terrain.
  • Do NOT: Stay in areas where a slide has already occurred; secondary slides can follow.
  • Do NOT: Enter buildings near steep slopes without professional inspection post-slide.

‚úÖ What TO DO Before a Landslide?

  • Check Local Risk: Understand if your area is prone to landslides via geological surveys.
  • Drainage Planning: Properly manage water flow on your property to reduce erosion.
  • Emergency Supplies: Keep a kit with essentials, including maps, flashlight, food, and water.
  • Vegetation: Plant deep-rooted vegetation on slopes to help stabilize soil.
  • Stay Alert: During heavy rain or after earthquakes, be vigilant for warning signs.
