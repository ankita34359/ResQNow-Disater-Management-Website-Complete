Understand droughts, learn how to prepare and reduce their impact on life and livelihood.

ðŸŒµ What is a Drought?

  • Definition: A prolonged period of abnormally low rainfall causing water shortage.
  • Types: Meteorological, Agricultural, Hydrological, Socioeconomic.
  • Duration: Can last from months to years.
  • Affected Areas: Arid and semi-arid regions, but can occur anywhere.
  • Impact: Affects crops, water supply, economy, health, and ecosystems.

â›” What NOT To Do During a Drought?

  • Do NOT: Waste water on non-essential activities like washing vehicles or watering lawns.
  • Do NOT: Delay action assuming rain will arrive soon.
  • Do NOT: Overuse underground water sources without monitoring.
  • Do NOT: Plant water-heavy crops in drought-prone areas.
  • Do NOT: Ignore water usage regulations or restrictions.

âœ… What TO DO Before a Drought?

  • Conserve Water: Practice water-saving techniques like low-flow faucets and efficient irrigation.
  • Rainwater Harvesting: Install systems to collect and store rainwater.
  • Drought-resistant Crops: Grow crops that need less water.
  • Soil Management: Improve soil moisture retention through mulching and organic content.
  • Awareness: Educate your community about drought mitigation techniques.

ðŸ’§ What TO DO During a Drought?

  • Use Water Wisely: Limit consumption, fix leaks, and avoid wastage.
  • Reuse: Collect grey water for non-potable uses like gardening or cleaning.
  • Monitor Usage: Keep track of daily water usage at home and in farms.
  • Stay Updated: Follow local advisories and drought alerts.
  • Community Efforts: Participate in neighborhood water-saving drives.

ðŸŒ± What TO DO After a Drought?

  • Rehydrate Soil: Use compost and mulch to restore soil health.
  • Rebuild Water Reserves: Refill tanks, ponds, and groundwater systems gradually.
  • Assess Damage: Inspect crop loss and infrastructure for repairs.
  • Learn & Improve: Review what worked and update your drought plan.
  • Support Others: Help farmers, families, and local initiatives in recovery efforts.
